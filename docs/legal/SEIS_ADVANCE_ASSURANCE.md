## Part 3 — Business Activity Narrative

*Draft text for HMRC "nature of trade" description. Adapt as needed before submission.*

---

SPIKE LAND LTD is a technology company developing a cloud-based software platform for developers and businesses.

**What the platform does**

The company is building a software-as-a-service (SaaS) platform that provides:

1. **Developer Tools** — A browser-based code editor with live preview capabilities, enabling developers to write, test, and deploy code directly from the browser. The platform includes real-time collaborative editing and on-demand code transpilation services.

2. **MCP (Model Context Protocol) Registry** — A registry and marketplace of over 80 AI-powered developer tools that integrate with modern AI assistants. These tools cover code review, image generation, data querying, and workflow automation.

3. **Edge Computing Infrastructure** — API services running on Cloudflare's global edge network, providing low-latency compute, database, and authentication services to platform users.

**How revenue is earned**

The company plans to generate revenue through:
- Subscription fees for premium platform access (SaaS model)
- Usage-based pricing for compute and API resources consumed
- Enterprise licensing for organisations requiring dedicated instances

Revenue is derived from providing active computing services, not from passive licensing of intellectual property. The platform requires continuous engineering, maintenance, and operational support — this is a genuine technology trade, not a royalties business.

**Customer profile**

Target customers are:
- Individual software developers and technical professionals
- Small-to-medium technology companies
- Enterprise development teams requiring collaborative tools

**Trade history**

The company was incorporated in December 2025. It is currently in the pre-revenue, development phase:
- Platform architecture is built and operational (running on Cloudflare Workers)
- Core features (code editor, real-time sync, MCP registry) are functional
- Revenue generation has not yet commenced at scale (minimal/testing revenue only)
- The company is preparing to trade and expects to begin generating meaningful revenue in 2026

**Why the trade qualifies**

The company's trade is software development and the provision of cloud computing services. This is an active technology trade that:
- Does not fall within any excluded activity category
- Requires ongoing technical development, customer support, and operational management
- Carries genuine commercial risk with the potential for significant growth
- Is not a scheme or arrangement designed to provide tax relief

## Part 7 — Key Risks

### Risk 1: Royalties Exclusion

**Risk**: HMRC could argue that SaaS subscription revenue constitutes "receiving royalties or licence fees" (an excluded activity).

**Mitigation**:
- HMRC's own guidance (VCM3010) distinguishes between passive licensing and active service provision
- SaaS revenue is payment for an active computing service, not a passive licence to use IP
- The company provides ongoing services: hosting, compute, support, updates, API access
- Customers do not receive a copy of software to install — they access a hosted service
- HMRC has consistently accepted SaaS businesses as qualifying trades in recent years
- The 2023 Finance Act did not change the treatment of SaaS businesses

**Assessment**: LOW RISK — Strong precedent for SaaS qualifying

### Risk 2: Financial Activities Exclusion

**Risk**: If the platform processes payments or handles financial data, HMRC could consider it a financial activity.

**Mitigation**:
- The platform is a developer tools platform, not a financial service
- Any payment processing is via third-party providers (Stripe) as a normal business function
- The company does not lend money, take deposits, or provide financial advice

**Assessment**: NEGLIGIBLE RISK

### Risk 3: Connected Services

**Risk**: Providing services substantially to connected persons.

**Mitigation**:
- The platform targets unconnected third-party developers and businesses
- No related-party transactions planned
- Director will not be a customer of the platform in a material capacity

**Assessment**: LOW RISK

### Risk 4: Risk-to-Capital

**Risk**: Since Finance Act 2018, HMRC applies a "risk-to-capital" condition — the company must demonstrate genuine risk of loss with the potential for investor gain.

**Mitigation**:
- The company is pre-revenue with genuine commercial uncertainty
- Significant product-market risk: unproven market, competition from established players
- Technology risk: platform requires ongoing development and scaling
- No asset-backed security — investment is pure equity risk
- Potential for significant gain through growth in platform adoption and revenue
- This is exactly the type of early-stage technology company SEIS was designed for

**Assessment**: LOW RISK — Pre-revenue tech startup is the archetypal SEIS company

### Risk 5: New Trade Condition

**Risk**: The "new qualifying trade" condition requires the trade to be less than 3 years old. If the company has been trading informally before incorporation, HMRC could argue the trade is older.

**Mitigation**:
- SPIKE LAND LTD was incorporated December 2025
- No predecessor company carried on this trade
- Any prior development was personal/hobby activity, not a trade
- No commercial sales have been made

**Assessment**: LOW RISK

### Risk 6: Readiness to Trade

**Risk**: HMRC may question whether the company is genuinely preparing to trade or is merely a dormant shell.

**Mitigation**:
- Platform is actively developed with functional code editor, MCP registry, edge services
- Infrastructure is deployed and operational on Cloudflare Workers
- Evidence: GitHub repositories, Cloudflare dashboard, platform demos
- Business plan shows clear path to revenue generation

**Assessment**: LOW RISK — Strong evidence of genuine trade preparation

### Risk 7: Director as Investor

**Risk**: Zoltan Erdos (sole director, 100% shareholder) investing personally would make them a "connected person" — their shares would not qualify for SEIS relief.

**Mitigation**:
- A director is not automatically connected — connection arises from 30%+ shareholding
- Currently 100% shareholder — any investment by Zoltan would be connected
- **Solution**: Only external, unconnected investors should subscribe for SEIS shares
- Post-investment, Zoltan's shareholding must dilute below 30% for him to be unconnected
- Even if Zoltan remains connected, external investors can still qualify if they are unconnected

**Assessment**: MEDIUM RISK — Ensure investment round is structured for external investors only. Zoltan should not claim SEIS relief on any personal investment while holding 30%+.

### Risk 8: Post-Assurance Changes

**Risk**: Advance assurance is based on the company's circumstances at the time of application. Material changes before share issue could invalidate the assurance.

**Mitigation**:
- Notify HMRC of any material changes (new trade activities, structural changes, etc.)
- Do not commence excluded activities
- Do not acquire subsidiaries or enter partnerships
- Keep HMRC informed if circumstances change

**Assessment**: LOW RISK — Maintain communication with HMRC

### Risk 9: £250,000 Lifetime Limit

**Risk**: SEIS investment is capped at £250,000 total per company (raised from £150,000 by Finance Act 2023). Exceeding this requires EIS qualification.

**Mitigation**:
- Plan the raise amount within the £250,000 SEIS limit
- If additional funding is needed, structure as a separate EIS round (requires separate advance assurance)
- 70% of SEIS funds must be spent before EIS investment can be received

**Assessment**: LOW RISK — Awareness and planning required
