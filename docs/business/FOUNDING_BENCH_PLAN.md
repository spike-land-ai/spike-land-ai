# Founding Bench Plan

> **Purpose**: build a real company after the first funding close without making
> vague pre-funding promises
> **Date**: 11 March 2026
> **Operating principle**: outputs first, titles second, equity last

---

## 1. Core Rule

Spike Land should not recruit around sentiment.

It should recruit around ownership of critical outputs after funding is secured.

That means:

- no broad equal equity split with former colleagues
- no "team slide" inflation before commitments exist
- no offers without capital, runway, scope, and start conditions

The right posture is:

> money in the bank first, then specific roles, specific deliverables, and
> vesting-backed offers

---

## 2. What The Company Actually Needs

The business does not need twenty people. It needs owners for four lanes:

1. **Platform hardening**  
   runtime quality, documentation, onboarding capacity, second technical owner

2. **Design partner conversion**  
   account targeting, outreach, discovery, pilot delivery, paid conversion

3. **Enterprise and regulated trust**  
   diligence, buyer credibility, architecture challenge, risk translation,
   higher-trust customer conversations

4. **Operating cadence**  
   hiring support, prioritisation, delivery rhythm, founder de-risking

If a person does not clearly strengthen one of those lanes, they should not be
part of the initial founding bench.

---

## 3. Team Categories

### A. Core builders

These are people who will carry real weekly responsibility.

Requirements:

- named lane
- weekly execution
- clear 30/60/90-day scorecard
- vesting
- IP assignment

This group should stay very small.

### B. Advisor-operators

These are people who will materially improve a lane but are not day-to-day
operators.

Requirements:

- narrow remit
- monthly or fortnightly cadence
- intro / diligence / review / hiring outputs
- smaller vesting grant or cash-plus-options later

### C. Specialist contributors

These are experts brought in for bounded projects.

Requirements:

- fixed brief
- fixed output
- cash-first where possible

## 5. Post-Funding Sequence

### Phase 1: first 14 days after funding

Objectives:

- confirm runway and hiring envelope
- define the first two roles
- prepare concrete offers

Outputs:

- budget by role
- lane owners defined
- offer templates ready

### Phase 2: days 15-30

Objectives:

- approach the highest-signal 3-5 people individually
- run working conversations, not ceremonial chats

Outputs:

- role-specific conversations completed
- interest level and availability known
- trial scopes defined

### Phase 3: days 30-60

Objectives:

- start with one or two people on real scoped work
- validate working style before finalizing long-term grants

Outputs:

- first scorecards active
- clear evidence of execution chemistry
- go / no-go on longer-term role

## 6. 30/60/90-Day Scorecards By Lane

### Lane 1: Platform hardening

30 days:

- audit core runtime risks
- identify top onboarding blockers
- produce architecture notes and runbooks

60 days:

- own one production-critical area without founder shadowing
- ship one meaningful hardening improvement

90 days:

- support customer onboarding independently
- reduce bus-factor risk materially

### Lane 2: Design partner conversion

30 days:

- build and qualify the target-account list
- source discovery calls

60 days:

- move qualified accounts into pilot discussions
- help close design partners

90 days:

- contribute directly to at least one paid conversion or clear path to paid

### Lane 3: Enterprise and regulated trust

30 days:

- pressure-test architecture and customer claims
- tighten diligence narrative

60 days:

- support technical walkthroughs and enterprise-facing conversations

90 days:

- materially improve the quality of one strategic relationship or diligence path

### Lane 4: Operating cadence

30 days:

- establish meeting rhythm, priorities, and role clarity

60 days:

- improve delivery predictability and decision speed

90 days:

- lower founder overload measurably

---

## 7. Offer Principles

Every post-funding offer should include:

- role
- lane
- expected weekly commitment
- first 90-day outputs
- cash compensation if any
- vesting structure if applicable
- start date

Every offer should avoid:

- vague founding language
- equal-share symbolism
- promises before budget and scope are real

---

## 8. Message Template

Use this after funding, not before:

> The round is closed and we have runway. I am building a small founding bench
> around Spike Land and I want to talk to you about a specific lane.
>
> The role I have in mind is [lane / title]. The first 90 days would be judged
> on [outputs]. The commitment level is [time]. Compensation would be [cash /
> equity / both], with vesting for any long-term grant.
>
> If that shape is interesting, let's discuss the role concretely.

---

## 9. Decision Filter

Before bringing anyone in, ask:

1. what lane do they own?
2. what output do they improve in 90 days?
3. are they truly operating or mainly advising?
4. would I still want them involved if no equity were mentioned in the first conversation?

If the answer to any of these is weak, stop there.

---

## 10. Summary

The right move is not to give away most of the company to avoid building alone.

The right move is:

- raise cleanly
- define the lanes
- recruit selectively
- pay in scope, ownership, and vesting after the role is real

That creates a real founding bench instead of a sentimental cap table.
